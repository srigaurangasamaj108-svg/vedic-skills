## About This Course

This course introduces the Bhagavad Gītā as a coherent
philosophical and spiritual text.

It focuses on:
- the structure of the dialogue
- key conceptual frameworks
- recurring themes across chapters

The course does **not** provide personal advice or life application.
Those belong to later stages of engagement.

---

## What This Course Covers

- The setting and structure of the Bhagavad Gītā  
- The relationship between action, knowledge, and devotion  
- Core concepts such as:
  - duty (dharma)
  - action (karma)
  - detachment (vairāgya)
  - knowledge of the self

## Canonical References

This course references verses from the Library, including:

- Bhagavad Gītā 2.47

Participants are encouraged to read the verses directly
in the Library alongside the course material.

---

## Who This Course Is For

- students beginning their study of the Bhagavad Gītā  
- readers seeking conceptual clarity  
- those who want structured understanding before application  

No prior background is required.

---

## Relationship to Guidance

This course provides **understanding**, not direction.

For personal or situational clarity,
Guidance pathways may be explored separately.
