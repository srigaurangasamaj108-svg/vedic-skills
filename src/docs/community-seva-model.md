# Community Seva Model  
*Vedic Skills*

---

## 1. Purpose of Community Seva

Community in Vedic Skills exists to express Vedic values through
**service, culture, and support**, not through authority or instruction.

Community is where wisdom is **lived together**, quietly and locally.

---

## 2. What Community Is (and Is Not)

Community **is**:
- relational
- participatory
- service-oriented
- locally grounded

Community **is not**:
- a teaching platform
- a counseling space
- a governance body
- a place for doctrinal authority

Community supports life;
it does not direct belief.

---

## 3. Seva Domains (Vibhāgas)

Community work is organized through **Seva Domains**, traditionally
referred to as *Vibhāgas*.

A Seva Domain is:
- a **field of service**
- not an administrative department
- not a hierarchy
- not an identity label

Seva Domains exist to **respond to real human needs** in a structured,
ethical manner.

---

## 4. Why “Departments” Is the Wrong Model

The term *department* implies:
- authority
- control
- reporting structures
- institutional power

Seva Domains deliberately avoid this framing.

They are:
- flexible
- service-driven
- context-sensitive
- volunteer-oriented

This preserves humility and prevents bureaucracy.

---

## 5. Scope of Seva Domains

Each Seva Domain:
- focuses on **one field of service**
- operates within clear ethical boundaries
- avoids overlap with Guidance or Study
- remains optional and non-intrusive

Examples of fields include:
- family wellbeing
- child development
- elder care
- education support
- food service
- cultural activities

Domains do not claim completeness.
They respond where service is possible.

---

## 6. Relationship to Guidance and Study

Seva Domains:
- may host study circles (Study content only)
- may refer individuals to Guidance when appropriate
- must never provide personal guidance themselves

This separation protects participants and facilitators alike.

---

## 7. Local Chapters and Autonomy

Seva Domains are expressed through **local chapters**.

Local chapters:
- choose which domains to activate
- adapt activities to cultural and legal context
- operate based on capacity and trust

There is no requirement to activate all domains.

Local autonomy is essential for authenticity.

---

## 8. Phase-Based Rollout

Seva Domains are introduced in phases:

- **Phase 1** — foundational, non-controversial domains  
- **Phase 2** — specialized or capacity-dependent domains  
- **Phase 3** — advanced or context-specific domains  

Phased rollout ensures:
- quality over quantity
- trust before expansion
- sustainability over symbolism

---

## 9. Safeguards and Boundaries

All Seva Domains must observe:
- respect for privacy
- voluntary participation
- no pressure or obligation
- no authority claims
- no ideological enforcement

Service must remain **free of coercion**.

---

## 10. Accountability and Review

Seva Domains:
- are reviewed periodically
- may be paused or retired
- evolve based on learning and context

Continuation is based on:
- usefulness
- ethical conduct
- community trust

---

## 11. Final Note

Community is the most delicate expression of wisdom.

When done correctly:
- it supports without controlling
- serves without labeling
- gathers without dividing

Seva is successful when it is **quiet, consistent, and kind**.
